# P&L Explain in Practice

## Why this chapter matters

P&L explain is the bridge between valuation and control. A desk may tolerate temporary model simplifications, but it
will not trust a platform whose day-over-day P&L cannot be reconciled. This chapter explains how to think about actual
P&L, theoretical P&L, explained versus unexplained P&L, and the operational causes of residuals.

## Notation used in this chapter

- $V_t$ is the end-of-day or intraday valuation at time $t$.
- $P\&L_{actual}$ is the realized accounting or economic P&L over the period.
- $P\&L_{theoretical}$ is the model-implied change in value under the chosen explain framework.
- $P\&L_{unexplained}$ is the residual after subtracting explained components from actual P&L.

## 1. Actual P&L and theoretical P&L

### 1.1 Actual P&L

Actual P&L is the observed economic or accounting change over the reporting interval. In a simplified form,
$$
P\&L_{actual}=V_t - V_{t-1} + CF_t
$$
where $CF_t$ represents realized cash movements such as coupons, premiums, fees, or settlements that are recognized over
the interval. The exact definition depends on desk accounting conventions and whether the report is economic,
accounting, clean, dirty, or funding-inclusive.

### 1.2 Theoretical P&L

Theoretical P&L is the model-based change implied by the market and trade state under the explain framework. A high-level
decomposition is
$$
P\&L_{theoretical}=P\&L_{market}+P\&L_{carry}+P\&L_{new\ trades}+P\&L_{cashflows}+P\&L_{model\ changes}+P\&L_{valuation\ adjustments}
$$
The purpose of P&L explain is to map actual P&L into economically interpretable components, not merely to report a
number.

## 2. Explained versus unexplained P&L

The residual is
$$
P\&L_{unexplained}=P\&L_{actual}-P\&L_{explained}
$$
where explained P&L is the sum of the components the platform chooses to model explicitly. A useful explain framework
keeps this residual small and stable. Persistent unexplained P&L is a control problem, not just a reporting nuisance.

Common root causes include:

- stale or incorrect market data,
- booking mismatches,
- wrong fixings or accruals,
- trade amendments not captured consistently,
- model-version differences,
- omitted valuation adjustments,
- approximation error from using Greeks instead of full revaluation.

## 3. Main explain components

### 3.1 Market-move P&L

Market-move P&L is the change caused by moving risk factors from yesterday's market state to today's market state while
holding the trade set otherwise fixed. A Greek approximation may write
$$
P\&L_{market} \approx \Delta\text{-}P\&L + \Gamma\text{-}P\&L + Vega\text{-}P\&L + \rho\text{-}P\&L + CS01\text{-}P\&L
$$
A full revaluation implementation computes
$$
P\&L_{market}=V(M_t,\mathcal{T}_{t-1})-V(M_{t-1},\mathcal{T}_{t-1})
$$
where $M_t$ is the market state and $\mathcal{T}_{t-1}$ is the previous trade set.

### 3.2 Carry and theta

Carry is the expected P&L generated by passage of time under unchanged market levels. For fixed-income products it is
often associated with accrual and financing effects; for options, theta is the classic time-decay term.

A schematic decomposition is
$$
P\&L_{carry}=V(M_{t-1},\mathcal{T}_{t-1};t)-V(M_{t-1},\mathcal{T}_{t-1};t-1)
$$
where the market state is held fixed but time advances. Carry and theta should usually be separated from pure market
moves because they have different economic interpretation.

### 3.3 New trades, unwinds, and amendments

Trade-flow P&L measures what changed because the trade inventory changed. One useful decomposition is
$$
P\&L_{trade\ flow}=V(M_t,\mathcal{T}_t)-V(M_t,\mathcal{T}_{t-1})
$$
This captures new trades, unwinds, novations, or corrected bookings. A platform that fails to isolate trade-flow P&L
will often misclassify business activity as market P&L.

### 3.4 Cashflows and fixings

A clean explain should separate contractual cash movements and fixing events. Examples include:

- coupon accrual and payment,
- bond principal redemption,
- swap fixing resets,
- option premium settlements,
- CDS premium accrual and settlement.

Fixings deserve special treatment because they can change valuation even when the broader market state appears unchanged.

### 3.5 Model changes

Model-change P&L arises when valuation inputs or methodologies change independently of the market. Examples include:

- curve-construction changes,
- volatility-surface interpolation changes,
- different recovery assumptions,
- revised day-count or schedule conventions,
- pricing-engine upgrades,
- revised calibration tolerances.

A simple control split is
$$
P\&L_{model\ change}=V(M_t,\mathcal{T}_t;\mathcal{M}_{new})-V(M_t,\mathcal{T}_t;\mathcal{M}_{old})
$$
where $\mathcal{M}$ denotes the model specification. Model-change P&L should never be silently mixed into market-move P&L.

### 3.6 Data issues

Data issues are a major source of explain failure. Examples include:

- missing quotes,
- stale market snapshots,
- wrong reference data,
- incorrect fixings,
- benchmark remappings,
- curve bootstraps built from incomplete or inconsistent nodes.

For this reason, a production explain engine should keep diagnostics alongside the report, not just the final numbers.

### 3.7 Valuation adjustments

Depending on the desk, a complete explain may include separate lines for valuation adjustments such as:

- liquidity reserves,
- bid-ask or exit adjustments,
- funding valuation adjustment,
- credit valuation adjustment,
- capital or reserve overlays.

These adjustments often move more slowly than market factors, but when they do move, they must be documented explicitly
or the unexplained residual will become misleading.

## 4. Actual control workflow

A good daily control workflow usually follows this order:

1. confirm the trade population,
2. confirm the market snapshot and curve/surface diagnostics,
3. compute explained P&L using a documented decomposition order,
4. analyze residuals by book, desk, and factor,
5. escalate persistent or concentrated unexplained P&L.

The decomposition order matters. For example, trade-flow effects should typically be stripped out before market-move
analysis so that business activity is not confused with market performance.

## 5. Approximation versus full revaluation

A Greek-based explain is fast and often useful, but it must be treated as an approximation. For products with strong
convexity, smile sensitivity, or jump-to-default exposure, full revaluation explains are more reliable. A robust
platform may therefore provide both:

- a fast approximate explain for intraday monitoring,
- a full revaluation explain for official end-of-day reporting.

The documentation should make the distinction explicit, not hidden in implementation detail.

## 6. Reporting design principles

A useful P&L explain report should support:

- decomposition by explain component,
- drill-down by trade, strategy, book, desk, or issuer,
- linkage to factor moves and market diagnostics,
- historical reproducibility from archived snapshots,
- reconciliation to official accounting or control reports.

The explain engine must therefore store lineage. Without the exact trade set, market state, model version, and
adjustment configuration used for the run, unexplained P&L cannot be investigated properly.
